# 🧠 swarmvault - Organize research into your private wiki

[![](https://img.shields.io/badge/Download_Swarmvault-Blue)](https://github.com/anitagraviti-sys/swarmvault)

Swarmvault helps you build a personal knowledge base. It processes raw research documents and saves them as markdown files. It builds a map of your information so you can find connections between notes. This tool works locally on your computer. Your files stay on your hard drive. 

The software uses artificial intelligence to scan your documents. It extract facts and creates links between related topics. This creates a knowledge graph. Over time, your vault grows into a searchable database that helps you track your projects and interests. You can connect it to AI tools like Claude Code to automate how you read and organize your library.

## 🚀 Setting up your vault

Once the installation finishes, open the Swarmvault icon on your desktop. The setup wizard guides you through the initial configuration.

1. Pick a folder on your computer where you want to store your notes. Choose a location you back up often.
2. Grant the application permission to index your files. This allows the software to read your markdown documents.
3. The software creates a local database. This file tracks the relationships between your notes.
4. If you use AI assistants, enter your API keys in the settings menu. You can skip this step if you choose to run tasks manually.
5. Click finish to open the main dashboard.

## 📖 How to use the interface

The dashboard shows a list of your files on the left. The center area displays the content of your selected note.

- Create a new note by clicking the plus icon.
- Type your notes in standard markdown format.
- Use hashtags to label your research areas. For example, add #ai-agents or #research to your document.
- The Knowledge Graph view shows dots connected by lines. Each dot is a note. Lines reveal how your topics relate to each other.
- Use the search bar at the top to find specific terms across your entire library.
- Hybrid search combines keyword matching with AI context. This helps you find relevant notes even if you do not use the exact words from the original document.

## 🔍 Understanding the technical features

Swarmvault organizes information through three main layers:

The primary layer acts as your document reader. It processes text files and stores them as markdown. This format keeps your data portable. You can open these files in any standard text editor.

The secondary layer maintains your knowledge graph. It constantly scans your notes for common themes. It links notes together based on your topics. This makes your vault feel like a second brain.

The third layer is the MCP server. This allows outside programs to read and write to your vault. If you use tools like Claude Code or Codex, they can update your notes as you work. This keeps your research up to date without manual input.

## 🛠️ Managing your library

Keep your vault clean by archiving old research. You can create subfolders for different projects. The software recognizes folder structures and includes them in the graph. 

If your machine gets slow, check the memory usage in the settings menu. You can limit how many files the AI processes at once. This keeps the application responsive on older hardware.

Back up your vault manually. Copy your source folder to an external drive or cloud storage provider. Since the files are plain text, you do not need special software to restore them. Just point the Swarmvault application to the copied folder in the settings menu to resume your work.

## ❓ Frequently asked questions

Do I need to be a developer to use this? No. The interface handles the logic behind the scenes. You only interact with your notes.

Does the software send my data to the cloud? No. All processing happens on your local machine. Your files remain on your disk.

Can I turn off the AI features? Yes. You can disable the automated linking if you prefer to manage connections manually.

What happens if I stop using the software? Your notes remain as safe, editable markdown files. They are not locked in a proprietary format.

Does the software support images? Yes. You can drag and drop images into your notes. The software stores them in a local folder alongside your text.
